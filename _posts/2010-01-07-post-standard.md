All cybersecurity professionals, except those who stand still, grow and adapt. They soon know that growth is vital, much like the way I learned this myself. When I first started my journey into cybersecurity at KCA University in Nairobi, I was captivated by the complexity and potential of the digital world. I recall a moment, much like Wendy in her garden, when a simple hands-on project sparked a passion that set the course for my career. From then on, I knew that to thrive in cybersecurity, continuous learning and curiosity were essential. Two is the beginning of the end, and in tech, every new step is a new beginning.

My first hands-on experience with AI training was like tidying up the mind—organizing knowledge, connecting concepts, and making sense of the chaos. Each evening, after classes or labs, I would review what I learned, sorting out new skills and understanding how they fit into the wider world of information security, systems audit, and digital forensics.

If you could stay awake through the night, you’d see a cybersecurity student—maybe me—reflecting on the day’s challenges, analyzing threats, reviewing logs, and learning from every success and mistake. Like tidying up drawers, this nightly habit helps transform confusion into understanding, and uncertainty into confidence. The vulnerabilities and risks discovered during the day are folded away, and new insights and solutions are placed at the top, ready for the next day’s work.

Have you ever seen a map of a security analyst’s mind? Experts sometimes try to diagram the flow of threats, responses, and defenses, but the reality is more intricate. The mind of a cybersecurity student is a constantly shifting landscape, filled with zigzag lines of learning, islands of specialization—from digital forensics to governance, risk, and compliance—splashes of new technologies, and rivers of data flowing through every problem and solution.

Of course, each cybersecurity journey is unique. One student might have a passion for AI and digital forensics, another for policy and audit. Some work in teams, others prefer solitary research. But there’s a family resemblance: curiosity, resilience, and a drive to build safer digital futures. On these shores of technology, we are always beaching our coracles, testing new tools and techniques, knowing that every challenge is an adventure.

The field of cybersecurity is snug and compact—not large and sprawly, but packed with opportunities for growth. In the light of day, it’s a game of logic and skill; in the quiet before sleep, it becomes a real battle against unseen threats. That’s why we keep our night-lights on—vigilant, prepared, and always learning.

Occasionally, in my studies and practice, I encounter concepts I don’t fully understand. AI, for example, once seemed mysterious, but now stands out boldly in my work, reshaping how I think about defense and innovation. Its presence in cybersecurity is growing, and as I continue my journey, I’m eager to explore its full potential.
